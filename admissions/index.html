    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the eligibility criteria for your programmes?

    For undergraduate programmes (B.Sc.), candidates must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with science stream (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics) with a minimum aggregate of 50% marks.

    What is the fee structure for various programmes?

    The fee structure can vary significantly based on the programmes or colleges. However, at Apollo Allied Health Academy, the fee structure for all programs is 2.5 LPA.

    Are there any scholarships or financial assistance available?

    Yes, we offer merit-based scholarships ranging from 10% to 25% of the tuition fee. Additionally, we have need-based financial assistance programs. Students can also avail educational loans from our partner banks with special interest rates. A separate scholarship application needs to be submitted along with supporting documents.

    What are the career prospects after completing these programmes?

    Our allied health graduates enjoy excellent employment opportunities in hospitals, diagnostic centres, research institutions, healthcare companies, and educational institutions. Many of our graduates are directly absorbed into Apollo Hospitals network. We have a dedicated placement cell that helps students with career guidance, interview preparation, and job placements. Our placement rate is consistently above 90%.

    Do you offer hostel facilities for students?

    Yes, we provide safe and comfortable hostel accommodations at all our centres. The hostels are equipped with modern amenities, 24/7 security and mess facilities. The hostel fee would be 1.5L to 2L per annum, including accommodation and meals. Limited seats are available, so early application is recommended.

    Is there an entrance exam for admission?

    No, there is no entrance exam required for admission to Apollo Allied Health Academy. The admission process is based on the aptitude test and interview. This makes the process easier for aspiring students.

    What is the academic calendar for the upcoming batch?

    The new academic batch is scheduled to start in September. You can visit the official website of the academy for more specific details about the start date and other calendar events.

    Do you provide practical training and internships?

    Absolutely! Practical training is an integral part of our curriculum. Students undergo regular clinical rotations at Apollo Hospitals and partner healthcare facilities. Final year includes a mandatory internship of 6 months. We also facilitate international internship opportunities for deserving students through our global healthcare partnerships.
