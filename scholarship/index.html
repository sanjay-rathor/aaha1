    Apollo Allied Health Academy – Scholarship Policy for Academic Session 2025-26

    Scholarships in academic fees will be granted to Class XII candidates from 2024-25 AY based on their percentage scores. Scholarships are designed to encourage academic excellence and support deserving students in their educational journey at Apollo Allied Health Academy

    Scholarship based on XII scores

    Scholarship Eligibility (for all boards such as CBSE/ICSE/State board)
    25% 95.1% & above Aggregate in XII
    20% 85.1% - 95% Aggregate in XII
    15% 75.1% - 85% Aggregate in XII
    10% 70% - 75% Aggregate in XII

    Scholarship based on XII scores

    Scholarship NEET Percentile
    75% 95 & Above
    50% 90.1-95
    25% 85-90

    The criteria for Apollo Allied Health Academy scholarship for the academic session 2025-26:

    • Merit-Based and First-Come-First Serve: The scholarships will be awarded purely based on merit and on a first-come-first-serve basis. This ensures that deserving students are given priority in receiving financial assistance.
    • Full Year Fee Submission: It is imperative that candidates availing of the scholarship must submit the full year's fee as a prerequisite. This ensures transparency and adherence to AAHA’s policies regarding financial commitments.
    • Apollo Group Employee Wards: 25% tuition fee waiver for only one child of any employee of Apollo Group or the partner University who secured Minimum 1st Class in +2.
    • Employee referrals: 10% tuition fee waiver for own siblings (brother/sister) of the Apollo / partner University employee referrals
    • Continuation Criteria: To sustain the tuition fee Scholarship/waiver in subsequent years.

    The scholarship will continue for the subsequent years provided the candidate.

    • Maintain a minimum of 80% attendance in each subject.
    • Secure a minimum CGPA of 7.5 at the end of the year in single attempt.
    • Conduct is Good i.e. not involved in any misbehavior act either in the campus or outside.
    • Not a beneficiary of any other scholarship scheme

    Terms & Conditions:


    • Document Verification: Scholarships are disbursed post verification of original documents. Failure to provide these within 3 months from the admission's last date may lead to non-awarding or cancellation of the scholarship.
    • Exclusions: Scholarships do not cover fees for backlogs, reappears, makeup fees, or other miscellaneous charges.
    • Misrepresentation: Concealing or misrepresenting information can lead to admission cancellation, scholarship withdrawal, and legal consequences.
    • Staff Errors: If over-awarded due to staff mistakes, candidates will receive the actual entitled amount. Excess funds may be reclaimed by the AAHA.
    • Authority: AAHA’s competent authority's decision is final and binding.
