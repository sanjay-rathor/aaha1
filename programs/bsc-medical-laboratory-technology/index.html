    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the eligibility criteria for this programme?
    Applicants must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects with a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate from a recognised board. Students from PCM background with Biology as an additional subject are also eligible.
    How is the programme structured?
    The 4-year programme is divided into six semesters with one year of job training. The first year covers foundational sciences, the second year focuses on core laboratory techniques, and the third year includes specialised diagnostic procedures and clinical internships. Each semester has both theory and practical components.
    Are scholarships available?
    Yes, Apollo Allied Health Academy offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 25% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds may qualify for special scholarships.
    What kind of placement support is provided?
    Our dedicated Career Development Cell offers comprehensive placement support, including resume building, interview preparation and hospital networking opportunities. We conduct clinical laboratory recruitment drives with 25+ institutions visiting the campus. Our 92% placement rate reflects our strong industry connections with hospitals and diagnostic centres.
    What certifications are included in the programme?
    The programme includes preparation for national and international certifications like DMLT (Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology) and ASCP (American Society for Clinical Pathology) international certification. Students also receive additional certifications in specialised techniques like PCR, ELISA and Flow Cytometry.
    Can I specialise in a specific laboratory field?
    Yes, in the fifth and sixth semesters, students can choose electives from specialised tracks including Clinical Biochemistry, Haematology, Microbiology, Histopathology, or Molecular Diagnostics. Your final research project and internship can also be aligned with your specialisation interest.
    What is the fee payment schedule?
    Fees are payable semester-wise. The first instalment is due at the time of admission and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options, including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts.
    What are the higher education opportunities after this programme?
    Graduates can pursue an M.Sc. in Medical Laboratory Technology, Clinical Research, Biochemistry, Microbiology, or other allied health sciences. Many of our students go on to specialise through post-graduate diplomas in specialised areas like Molecular Biology, Cytogenetics, or Blood Banking. About 30% of our graduates pursue higher education within 2 years of graduation.
    What is the fee for the programme?
    The tuition fee for the programme is 2.5 lakh per annum.
